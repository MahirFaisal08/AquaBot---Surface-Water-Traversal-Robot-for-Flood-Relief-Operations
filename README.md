## Overview
**AquaBot** is a surface water traversal robot designed to assist in **flood relief operations**, **underwater inspection**, and **environmental monitoring**.  
It enables safe exploration and data collection in aquatic environments that may be dangerous or inaccessible to humans.

The robot is capable of performing **autonomous navigation**, **inspection**, and **maintenance** tasks ‚Äî supporting flood rescue teams by surveying submerged areas and collecting vital information.

## üîß Setup & Working

- The main body structure was built using **cockshit** (lightweight floating material).  
- A **servo motor** and **Zed engine** were placed inside the robot body for propulsion and steering.  
- The **receiver**, connected to the **FlySky transmitter**, provides 6 control channels for movement and stability.  
- **ESC (Electronic Speed Controller)** regulates the motor speed and connects through channel 2.  
- The **servo motor**, connected to channel 4, controls the Zed engine direction.  
- The **metal gear shaft** ensures smooth torque transfer between motor and propeller.  

---

## Challenges Faced

- The **ESC burnt out** after prolonged operation due to overheating.  
- Balancing waterproofing with heat dissipation proved challenging.  
- Maintaining signal stability during high-frequency transmission required tuning.
